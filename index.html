About Me

Growing up in Bangladesh fostered a deep appreciation for community and problem-solving, shaping my mission to bridge advanced genomics and accessible healthcare. I believe transformative innovation thrives at the intersection of science, technology, and collaboration.

My academic path includes a BSc. (Honours) in Biochemistry & Molecular Biology from the University of Dhaka (Bangladesh), followed by an MSc. and a Dr. rer. nat. (PhD, Summa Cum Laude) in Neuroscience via the IMPRS program at the University of GÃ¶ttingen (Germany) & University of Bordeaux (France), originally supported by an Erasmus Mundus Masters scholarship. My doctoral research focused on epigenomic and transcriptomic analysis of developing, adult and aging brain: mechanisms of brain folding, neuronal function and finding novel therapy for dementia.

Currently, as a Research Associate at Evotec in Hamburg, I manage custom genomics solutions (NGS, Molecular Biology, Computational Biology) for pharmaceutical partners, aiming to accelerate drug discovery and tackle unmet medical needs.

Beyond the lab, I'm committed to community building and mentorship. I serve as a Bangla Language Trainer at Bornomala Bangla School here in Hamburg and as a Business Development Lead & Career Expert at Study and Career Coach Germany (SCCG), guiding students and professionals in life sciences. My goal is to leverage genomics, computational biology, and AI to catalyze innovations for accessible healthcare.
